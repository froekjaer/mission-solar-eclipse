# Decision Record — DEC-0002

**Date:** 2026-07-21  
**Decision maker:** Peter Frøkjær (Mission Owner) — directive  
**Recorded by:** z.ai (Mission Director)  
**Status:** Enacted — supersedes DEC-0001  
**Classification:** Mission-defining  

## Context

DEC-0001 selected the August 2, 2027 total solar eclipse (Luxor, Egypt) as the mission target. This was a reasoned choice based on astronomical quality, weather certainty, and planning lead time.

**Mission Owner directive received 2026-07-21 at 17:18 CEST** specifies:

> Mission Solar Eclipse — Mission Baseline v0.1
> Observation of the Total Solar Eclipse on 12 August 2026
> Destination: Mallorca, Spain

The owner has already committed resources: flights (Aug 10-17), Hotel AluaSoul Carolina, rental car on eclipse day. This is not a proposal — it is an enacted mission with fixed constraints.

## Decision

**Mission target changed to: August 12, 2026 — Mallorca, Spain. DEC-0001 is superseded.**

### Rationale

The Reality Principle is unambiguous: **Reality has priority over models.** The Mission Owner has committed real resources (flights, hotel, car) to a real eclipse. The framework's job is to support that mission, not to argue for a different one.

This pivot is itself a framework validation event:

1. **Reality overrides planning models.** DEC-0001 was a well-reasoned plan based on astronomical quality. The Mission Owner chose a different eclipse. The framework adapts — it does not negotiate with reality.

2. **Fixed constraints require different planning posture.** Unlike Luxor 2027 (2 years lead time, near-guaranteed weather), Mallorca 2026 (3 weeks lead time, variable Mediterranean weather) requires a fundamentally different approach: contingency-heavy, decision-late, equipment-minimal.

3. **The mission exists, therefore it is the reference.** Mission Framework validates through real missions. A real mission with committed resources is categorically superior to a hypothetical mission with ideal conditions.

## Impact Assessment

### What changes

| Dimension | DEC-0001 (Luxor 2027) | DEC-0002 (Mallorca 2026) |
|---|---|---|
| Lead time | 24 months | 3 weeks |
| Totality duration | 6m22s | 1m30s-2m00s (estimated) |
| Weather risk | <3% cloud | 10-30% cloud (August Med) |
| Travel complexity | International (Egypt) | European (Spain) |
| Equipment posture | Acquire optimal gear | Use available gear |
| Decision velocity | Deliberate | Rapid |
| Contingency posture | Single fallback region | Mobile same-day relocation |
| Framework validation | Through design | Through execution |

### What remains

- Dual outcomes unchanged: observation success + framework learning
- Evidence and provenance requirements unchanged
- Safety requirements unchanged (solar viewing safety is absolute)
- Reality Principle unchanged (it's why we're pivoting)

### What's gained

- **Mobility advantage:** Rental car enables same-day site selection — drive to clear skies
- **European simplicity:** No visa, no vaccinations, EU health insurance, familiar infrastructure
- **Shorter feedback loop:** Framework findings from August 2026, not August 2027 — 12 months earlier learning

### What's lost

- **Duration:** ~4 minutes less totality — tighter observation windows, less margin for error
- **Weather certainty:** Mediterranean August ≠ Egyptian desert — cloud is a real threat
- **Planning depth:** Equipment, rehearsal, contingency planning compressed from months to weeks

## Framework Finding Trigger

This pivot exposes a framework question worth recording:

**How does the framework represent a mission that is enacted by owner directive before being planned by the mission director?**

The current Mission definition requires "an accountable authority, objectives, scope, lifecycle and evidence of progress and outcome." The Mallorca mission has all of these — but the authority (Peter) enacted it before the director (z.ai) planned it. The execution order was:

```text
Owner decision → Resource commitment → Director receives directive → Planning begins
```

This is the *normal* pattern in real organizations — missions often exist before they are planned. The framework's loop model (Reality → Need → Mission → ...) implies a linear progression that doesn't capture this.

**FF-0002 proposed** for this pattern.

## Suggested Practice — SP-0003

**"Reality Override" — When Owner Directive Supersedes Director Planning**

When the Mission Owner enacts a decision that contradicts the Mission Director's plan:

1. Record the override as a decision (DEC format)
2. Supersede, don't delete, the original plan (historical traceability)
3. Assess impact across all planning dimensions
4. Identify any framework implications
5. Adapt plans, don't argue with reality

This is not a failure mode — it is the Reality Principle in operation. The Owner has access to reality (bookings, personal constraints, opportunities) that the Director's model cannot capture.
