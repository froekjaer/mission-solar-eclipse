## Title

**Structured Decision Record with Alternatives Table**

## Context

During eclipse target selection (DEC-0001), I needed to document a consequential decision among multiple alternatives with differing attributes. I created a structured format with: context, alternatives comparison table, explicit decision, rationale, consequences, evidence sources, and approval checklist.

## Practice Description

When recording a consequential mission decision:

1. **State context** — what must be decided and why now
2. **List alternatives** — including the "do nothing" or "defer" option
3. **Compare systematically** — use a table with consistent criteria across alternatives
4. **Record the decision** — explicit, unambiguous
5. **Provide rationale** — narrative justification
6. **List consequences** — immediate and downstream effects
7. **Cite evidence** — traceable sources that informed the decision
8. **Include approval** — review checklist with accountable authority

## Why This Could Be a General Mission Framework Practice

The Mission Framework Glossary defines Decision as "An authorised selection among alternatives, including a decision to act, not act, continue, stop, delegate or revise." The glossary also requires that "A consequential Decision must identify its accountable authority, time, basis and applicable policy."

This template operationalizes those requirements. It turns the Glossary's normative statements into a reproducible recording pattern. If standardized, it would:

- Ensure decisions are traceable (Dependency Descent compatible)
- Preserve the reasoning at decision time (not reconstructed later)
- Make alternatives visible (reducing hindsight bias)
- Enable cross-mission comparison of decision quality
